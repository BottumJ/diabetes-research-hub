## Breaking News (Web Search — March 21-28, 2026)

### Major FDA Actions

**Awiqli (Insulin Icodec) — APPROVED March 26, 2026**
Novo Nordisk's once-weekly basal insulin received FDA approval — the first and only once-weekly insulin for adults with T2D. Reduces injection burden from 7/week to 1/week. *Evidence level: Regulatory action (G).*

**Wegovy HD (Semaglutide 7.2 mg) — APPROVED March 19, 2026**
Higher-dose semaglutide approved for weight management in adults with obesity who tolerate 2.4 mg. Achieved ~21% mean weight loss at 72 weeks. *Evidence level: Regulatory action (G).*

### Key Clinical Data

**Retatrutide Phase 3 Results (Eli Lilly) — March 19, 2026**
Positive results from TRANSCEND-T2D-1 trial: up to 2.0% A1C reduction and 16.8% weight loss vs. placebo. This is a GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist — the first in its class with Phase 3 data. *Evidence level: 1b (Phase 3 RCT). BRONZE validation (single source — awaiting peer-reviewed publication).*

### Research Advances

**MIT Implantable Islet Cell Device — March 26, 2026**
New device encapsulates islet cells with an on-board oxygen generator, enabling cells to survive 3+ months and produce insulin without immunosuppression. Preclinical stage. *Evidence level: 5 (preclinical). Per Doctrine: "Demonstrated in animal model. Human relevance unconfirmed."*

**Two-Part Cell Therapy for T1D — March 2, 2026**
Researchers developing lab-made insulin-producing cells paired with custom-engineered protective immune cells to eliminate immunosuppression need. Backed by $1M funding. *Evidence level: 5 (early-stage research).*

**ATTD 2026 Conference (March 11-14)**
Dexcom G7 long-term data showed support for weight management and A1C lowering in T2D patients not on insulin. *Evidence level: 2b (registry data).*

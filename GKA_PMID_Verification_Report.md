### 1. DORZAGLIATIN (HMS5552) PHASE 3 TRIAL EFFICACY DATA

#### SEED Trial (Drug-Naïve Patients)
- **PMID:** 35551294
- **First Author:** Zhu
- **Journal:** Nature Medicine
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** Dorzagliatin in drug-naïve patients with type 2 diabetes: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial
- **Supports Claims:** HbA1c reduction -1.07% vs placebo -0.50% at week 24; improved glycemic control with good tolerability and safety profile; no severe hypoglycemia events

#### DAWN Trial (Add-On to Metformin)
- **PMID:** 35551292
- **First Author:** Yang
- **Journal:** Nature Medicine
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** Dorzagliatin add-on therapy to metformin in patients with type 2 diabetes: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial
- **Supports Claims:** HbA1c reduction -1.02% vs placebo -0.36% at week 24; effective glycemic control when combined with metformin; safe tolerability profile; no severe hypoglycemia

#### Dorzagliatin Meta-Analysis and Trial Sequential Analysis
- **PMID:** 36505359
- **First Author:** Yu
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Cardiovascular Medicine
- **Year:** 2022
- **Title:** Efficacy and safety of dorzagliatin for type 2 diabetes mellitus: A meta-analysis and trial sequential analysis
- **Supports Claims:** Meta-analysis showing HbA1c reduction 0.65%, FPG reduction 9.22 mg/dL, improved beta-cell function; remission data from phase II/III trials

### 2. GLUCOKINASE ACTIVATOR MECHANISM OF ACTION

#### Glucokinase as Glucose Sensor (Seminal Review)
- **PMID:** 2189759
- **First Author:** Matschinsky
- **Journal:** Diabetes
- **Year:** 1990
- **Title:** Glucokinase as glucose sensor and metabolic signal generator in pancreatic beta-cells and hepatocytes
- **Supports Claims:** Foundational evidence that glucokinase acts as glucose sensor in pancreatic beta cells; explains capacity, specificity, and affinities for glucose metabolism; glucose-dependent insulin secretion; hepatic role in glucose regulation

#### Exploring GKA Mechanisms In Vivo
- **PMID:** 21051584
- **First Author:** Perseghin
- **Journal:** Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism
- **Year:** 2010
- **Title:** Exploring the in vivo mechanisms of action of glucokinase activators in type 2 diabetes
- **Supports Claims:** GKAs lower blood glucose through effects in both liver and pancreas; glucokinase plays role as glucose sensor; allosteric activation mechanism

#### Molecular Targeting of GK-GKRP Pathway
- **PMID:** 25324018
- **First Author:** Hale
- **Journal:** Expert Opinion on Therapeutic Targets
- **Year:** 2015
- **Title:** Molecular targeting of the GK-GKRP pathway in diabetes
- **Supports Claims:** GK-GKRP complex regulation; dual mechanisms through pancreatic and hepatic glucokinase activation; hepatocyte-specific activation pathways

### 3. GKA HYPOGLYCEMIA RISK AND SAFETY CONCERNS

#### Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of GKA Safety (2023)
- **PMID:** 37223016
- **First Author:** Yang
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Endocrinology (Lausanne)
- **Year:** 2023
- **Title:** Evaluation of efficacy and safety of glucokinase activators-a systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Supports Claims:** Analysis of 13 RCTs (2,748 treated, 2,681 control); hypoglycemia risk (OR 1.88 vs placebo); dyslipidemia concerns; safety comparison of older vs newer GKAs; discusses glucokinase paradox

#### Overall Safety Evaluation Meta-Analysis (2024)
- **PMID:** 39629068
- **First Author:** Liang
- **Journal:** Diabetes, Metabolic Syndrome and Obesity
- **Year:** 2024
- **Title:** Evaluating the Overall Safety of Glucokinase Activators in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
- **Supports Claims:** 17 RCTs (4,918 patients); GKAs associated with higher risk of adverse events, hyperlipidemia, hyperuricemia; safety profile varies by agent type; older GKAs (MK-0941) had higher hypoglycemia vs newer agents like dorzagliatin

#### GKA Efficacy and Safety Meta-Analysis (2021)
- **PMID:** 34622877
- **First Author:** Gao
- **Journal:** Medicine (Baltimore)
- **Year:** 2021
- **Title:** The efficacy and safety of glucokinase activators for the treatment of type-2 diabetes mellitus: A meta-analysis
- **Supports Claims:** HbA1c reduction -0.3%; safety analysis including older-generation GKA concerns

## Notes on Verification

All PMIDs were verified through direct PubMed access and extraction of publication metadata. Each citation includes:
- Correct PMID (verified via pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov)
- First author last name
- Journal name (standard abbreviations)
- Publication year
- Brief title confirmation
- Claims directly supported by each publication

**Gaps Identified:**
- Specific cost-effectiveness studies comparing GKAs to other newer diabetes drugs are limited in published literature
- GKA-specific economic burden analysis papers are emerging (2023-2024) but were not found in PubMed during search
- Some claims about GKA pricing in China may require industry reports or regulatory filings rather than peer-reviewed literature

## Claims with Weaker PubMed Support

### 1. GKA-Specific Cost-Effectiveness Analysis
**Status:** Limited peer-reviewed literature

**What was found:**
- General diabetes drug cost-effectiveness (PMID 38639547) covers SGLT2i and GLP-1 agonists
- No specific cost-effectiveness analyses of GKAs vs other diabetes drugs in published MEDLINE/PubMed
- Market projections (GKA market $2.5B in 2025, reaching $8B by 2033) found only in industry reports, not peer-reviewed literature

**Recommendation for Dashboard:**
- Use PMID 38639547 for general newer drug cost-effectiveness framework
- Consider health economics publications from Chinese sources (Hua Medicine-sponsored analyses)
- Reference regulatory and reimbursement data rather than claiming peer-reviewed cost-effectiveness studies

### 2. Biosimilar/Generic Impact on Diabetes Drug Costs
**Status:** Information exists but PMID verification incomplete

**What was found:**
- Yale School of Medicine study showing biosimilars can be manufactured at 97% less than market price
- BPCIA (Biologics Price Competition and Innovation Act) impact: $37 billion saved
- Specific PMID for Yale research not directly captured in search results

**Recommendation for Dashboard:**
- The insulin cost analysis (PMID 34763823) addresses general cost barriers
- For biosimilar-specific claims, cite the Yale Medicine published research directly with DOI
- PMIDs 34763823 and general drug economics papers provide supporting context

### 3. GKA Pricing in China/NMPA Reimbursement Context
**Status:** Press releases and regulatory documents, not peer-reviewed literature

**What was found:**
- Dorzagliatin NMPA approval September 2022 (PMID 36449148, 38783768)
- NRDL (China National Reimbursement Drug List) inclusion effective January 1, 2024
- No peer-reviewed PubMed articles specifically on NMPA pricing or reimbursement rates
- Information from Hua Medicine press releases and company announcements

**Recommendation for Dashboard:**
- Use PMID 36449148 and PMID 38783768 for approval and development history
- Supplement with direct references to NMPA decision documents
- Reference NRDL inclusion as regulatory/reimbursement milestone (not peer-reviewed source)
- Add disclaimer that pricing data from regulatory sources, not published health economics

### 4. Early GKA Safety Concerns (MK-0941, Older Agents Hypoglycemia Paradox)
**Status:** Well-documented in literature but scattered across multiple sources

**What was found:**
- PMID 37223016 and PMID 39629068 address comparative safety of different GKAs
- Discussion of MK-0941 failures and GSIS disruption in search results, but specific PMIDs not captured
- Glucokinase paradox (low affinity should prevent hypoglycemia, but early GKAs caused it) mentioned in multiple papers

**Recommendation for Dashboard:**
- Use PMID 37223016 and PMID 39629068 for systemic safety analysis comparing GKA generations
- Include both as sources for "older GKA hypoglycemia concerns"
- These meta-analyses synthesize the historical safety literature including MK-0941 data

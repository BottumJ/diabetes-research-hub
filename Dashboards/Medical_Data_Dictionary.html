AMPK
AMP-activated protein kinase. A cellular energy sensor. Metformin activates AMPK, which suppresses inflammation and improves insulin sensitivity.
Serine/threonine kinase phosphorylating downstream targets (ACC, mTORC1, ULK1). Activated by AMP/ATP ratio, metformin. Central metabolic regulator.
Body System(s):
Blood & Vascular, Fat Tissue
Key Indicators:
AMPK phosphorylation, ACC phosphorylation
Normal Range:
Basal activity depends on energy status
In Diabetes:
Reduced AMPK activity in obesity/IR; metformin reactivates it
Connected To:
Metformin, Autophagy, mTOR, Metabolic health

Adiponectin
A hormone made by fat cells. Higher adiponectin levels protect against insulin resistance. Levels are low in obese people.
30 kDa adipokine. Produced by white adipose tissue. Enhances insulin sensitivity via AMPK activation, reduces inflammation, promotes glucose oxidation.

In Diabetes:

The receptor on cells that adiponectin (good hormone from fat) binds to. Activates beneficial pathways like AMPK.
AdipoR1 and AdipoR2. AdipoR1: skeletal muscle (glucose utilization); AdipoR2: liver (hepatic IR). Binds adiponectin; activates AMPK, PPAR-delta.

Antibodies (immune proteins) that attack the body's own cells. In T1D, multiple autoantibodies attack beta cells.

mTOR
Mechanistic target of rapamycin. A growth regulator. When inhibited (rapamycin), mTOR inhibition promotes Treg differentiation and transplant tolerance.
Serine/threonine kinase forming two complexes: mTORC1 (nutrient/growth) and mTORC2 (proliferation/survival). mTORC1 suppression inhibits protein synthesis, activates autophagy, promotes Treg.
Body System(s):
Immune System, Blood & Vascular
Key Indicators:
S6K phosphorylation (mTORC1 activity), 4E-BP1 phosphorylation
Normal Range:
Active (fed state); suppressed (fasting)
In Diabetes:
mTORC1 hyperactivation in T2D; inhibition promotes islet transplant tolerance
Connected To:
Rapamycin, Regulatory T Cell, Autophagy, Transplant
Gap Relevance:
Gap 3, Gap 5, Gap 6, Gap 8

Islet Transplant Challenge

Donor islet survival vs immune rejection

Pathway Sequence:

Donor Pancreas
Healthy islets harvested
→
Transplant
Infused into liver portal vein
→
Innate Immunity
Immediate blood-mediated inflammatory reaction
→
T Cell Recognition
Host CD8+ and CD4+ cells recognize donor MHC
→
Rejection Without IS
Immune attack destroys islets
→
Immunosuppression
Tacrolimus + Mycophenolate + Sirolimus
→
Tacrolimus Paradox
Calcineurin inhibition blocks NFAT -> insulin dysfunction
→
Insulin Independence
61% at 1yr (Edmonton); 8% at 20yr
Intervention Points: Therapeutic Strategies:
Belatacept: Costimulation blockade (calcineurin-sparing)
Rapamycin: mTORC1 inhibition (promotes Treg)
CAR-Treg: Engineered Tregs specific to donor antigen
Stem Cell Replacement: VX-880, Donislecel (less immunogenic)
Research Challenge: Gap 3: How to avoid calcineurin/NFAT toxicity while maintaining graft tolerance
Neuropathy Cascade

Hyperglycemia leads to nerve damage via inflammation

Pathway Sequence:

Hyperglycemia
High blood glucose sustained
→
ROS Surge
Reactive oxygen species accumulate in neurons
→
NF-kB Activation
ROS activate NF-kB in Schwann cells/axons
→
Cytokine Induction
IL-1beta, TNF-alpha, IL-6 produced
→
Schwann Cell Apoptosis
Schwann cells die (caspase-3 activation)
→
Myelin Breakdown
Myelin sheath degenerates
→
Axon Degeneration
Axons degenerate without myelin support
→
Neuropathy Symptoms
Numbness, pain, impaired sensation
Intervention Points:
Glycemic Control: Reduce hyperglycemia -> reduce ROS
Antioxidants: Scavenge ROS (ACE inhibitors, statins)
NF-kB Inhibitor: Block inflammation (experimental)
IL-1 Inhibitor: Anakinra (beta cell protective, neuroprotective)
Treg Enhancement: IL-10-producing Tregs (anti-inflammatory)
SGLT2i: Reduces ROS, suppresses Th17 (pro-inflammatory)
GLP-1 RA: Anti-inflammatory, may slow progression
Reversibility: Early intervention may reverse; late stages: permanent

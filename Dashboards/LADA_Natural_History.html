Gap #1 SILVER Validated) — C-Peptide Decline Trajectories & Intervention Windows

What This Dashboard Answers

LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults) is an autoimmune form of diabetes that is misdiagnosed as Type 2 in 80-90% of cases. Unlike classic Type 1, beta cell destruction in LADA is slower, creating a window where intervention could preserve remaining insulin production. This dashboard models how fast that window closes, what predicts faster progression, and which therapies map to which stage of decline.

Key Finding

LADA follows a biphasic C-peptide decline: rapid early loss (~55 pmol/L/year for years 0-5), then slower chronic loss (~20 pmol/L/year). Patients with high GADA titers (>180 U/mL) progress to insulin dependence in ~5 years. Low-titer patients may retain function for >10 years. This distinction determines which interventions are viable and when.

How to Use This

For researchers: the intervention window analysis (Tab 3) maps drug classes to disease stages. For clinicians: the autoantibody stratification (Tab 2) identifies which patients need the most urgent monitoring. For funders: the evidence gaps in each tab highlight where new trials would have the highest impact. This is a decision-support framework, not a treatment recommendation.

What This Cannot Tell You

C-peptide trajectories are population averages derived from cohort studies (ACTION LADA, HUNT, Botnia, Chinese cohorts). Individual patients vary substantially. Intervention window estimates are theoretical and untested in RCTs. This model does not account for genetic heterogeneity beyond HLA-DR3/DR4 classification.

Autoantibody Risk Stratification

How autoantibodies predict beta-cell decline rate and time to insulin dependency.

GADA Titer Thresholds

GADA (Glutamic Acid Decarboxylase Autoantibody) titer separates LADA phenotypes:

  • GADA >= 180 U/mL (LADA1): Strong autoimmunity, rapid progression
  • GADA < 180 U/mL (LADA2): Borderline autoimmunity, slower decline
  • 84% GADA+ require insulin by 6 years (UKPDS, PMID:9742976)

Risk Categories & Time-to-Insulin

Key Evidence

  • HUNT Study: HR 6.40 for low C-peptide; HR 5.37 for high GADA titer (PMID:34318969)
  • ACTION LADA: 8.8% LADA prevalence in adult-onset diabetes (n=6,156) (Hawa et al. 2013, PMID:23835333)
  • IA-2A positivity: Accelerates progression ~2-3 years earlier than GADA alone

Intervention Windows

Mapping therapeutic opportunities across the natural history of LADA.

Three Therapeutic Windows

  • SGLT2i + DPP4i
  • Metabolic optimization

Window 3: Late Phase

Duration: 5+ years post-diagnosis
C-Peptide Range: <0.3 nmol/L
Opportunity: Insulin required; focus on complication prevention
Therapeutic Strategies:
  • Intensive insulin therapy
  • Cardiovascular risk reduction
  • Nephroprotection

Vaccine & Immunotherapy Evidence

GAD-alum Vaccine (monotherapy — contradicted in T1D): Earlier DIAGNODE-2 and 2022 IPD meta-analysis suggested a HLA-DR3-DQ2+ subgroup signal of dose-dependent C-peptide preservation (PMID:32754804), but the confirmatory DIAGNODE-3 Phase 3 (n=174 HLA DR3-DQ2-enriched, recent-onset T1D) met pre-specified futility criteria at the March 2026 interim analysis and Diamyd announced discontinuation of monotherapy development in April 2026. LADA-specific pivotal data remain absent. Combination strategies (GAD-alum + vitamin D + etanercept) are still under investigation but unproven.

  • Historical signal (pre-DIAGNODE-3): HLA-DR3-DQ2 positive, baseline C-peptide >0.4 nmol/L, initiated within 2 years of diagnosis
  • Status April 2026: monotherapy contradicted in recent-onset T1D; effect in LADA not established in a confirmatory RCT

DPP4i + Early Insulin: Sitagliptin combined with insulin therapy preserves C-peptide better than insulin monotherapy in early LADA.

SGLT2i Combinations: SGLT2 inhibitor + DPP4 inhibitor combinations under investigation for beta-cell preservation.

Therapeutic Trial Evidence

  • GAD-alum vaccine: DIAGNODE-3 Phase 3 (n=174, HLA DR3-DQ2-enriched recent-onset T1D) met pre-specified futility criteria at the March 2026 interim and was discontinued; earlier DIAGNODE-2 (PMID 34299352) and 2022 IPD meta-analysis (PMID 35491968) had suggested an HLA DR3-DQ2 subgroup signal that did not replicate in the confirmatory trial. LADA-specific pivotal data absent. Monotherapy path contradicted; combination (GAD + vitamin D + etanercept) still exploratory.
  • Sitagliptin + Insulin: Early combination preserves C-peptide vs insulin monotherapy
  • Immune checkpoint approaches: Ongoing trials with anti-PD-1, anti-CTLA-4 in early LADA
  • IL-21 pathway modulation: Early-stage investigation for beta-cell protection
  • GLP-1 agonists: Emerging evidence for beta-cell preservation in LADA

Limitations

  • Computational model based on published cohort means, not individual patient-level data
  • C-peptide trajectories are approximations; actual decline rates vary by individual
  • LADA1/LADA2 dichotomy is a simplification of a continuous spectrum
  • Limited to GAD65 antibody-positive LADA; other autoantibody patterns may differ
  • Intervention window estimates are theoretical and untested in RCTs

Supply Chain Geography vs. Global Diabetes Burden - Gap #2: GOLD Validated

Data: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, IDF Diabetes Atlas 11th Ed. (2024), Company filings | Snapshot: 2026-03-15

What This Dashboard Answers

Advanced beta cell therapies (stem cell-derived islets, encapsulated cells, immunomodulation protocols) are in clinical development, but their projected availability maps onto existing healthcare inequities. This dashboard overlays the global supply chain for these therapies against the geographic distribution of diabetes burden, highlighting where the gap between need and access is widest.

How to Use This

For health systems researchers: quantifies supply-demand mismatch by country tier. For therapy developers: identifies markets and populations currently invisible to their distribution planning. For advocacy groups: provides data-backed evidence of projected access inequities before therapies reach market.

What This Cannot Tell You

Supply projections are modeled estimates, not confirmed manufacturing plans. Therapy development timelines are uncertain. Country-level infrastructure assessments are based on proxy indicators (cold chain, regulatory readiness) rather than direct capability surveys.

C. Supply Chain Requirements

  • Manufacturing: cGMP stem cell differentiation facilities ($50-100M to build)
  • Delivery: Hepatic portal vein infusion requires interventional radiology
  • Post-transplant: Lifelong immunosuppression monitoring (except encapsulated approaches)
  • Regulatory: FDA BLA pathway (US), EMA ATMP (EU), each country needs regulatory approval
  • Cold chain: Cells must be transported fresh or cryopreserved
  • Trained personnel: Interventional radiologists, transplant hepatologists, endocrinologists

B. Key Findings

  1. 81% of global diabetes burden is in low- and middle-income countries, but 88% of beta cell therapy trial sites are in high-income countries. Source: IDF Diabetes Atlas 11th Ed.
  2. India has 89.8M people with diabetes (2nd globally) and ZERO beta cell therapy trial sites. This represents the largest single equity gap.
  3. 7 of 8 major therapy developers are headquartered in the United States. This concentrates intellectual property and pricing power in a single health system.
  4. The top 5 countries by trial sites (US, Canada, France, Brazil, Italy) represent only 18% of global diabetes burden. Trial access is not aligned with clinical need.
  5. The bottom 20 countries by trial access represent 55% of global diabetes burden. The gap is acute in South/Southeast Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and MENA regions.
  6. Manufacturing requires $50-100M cGMP facilities - currently concentrated in Boston, Portsmouth NH, Bothell WA, Germantown MD, Bordeaux. Decentralized manufacturing is not yet planned.
  7. At current trajectory, initial pricing (~$500K based on CAR-T analogy) would be unaffordable for 80%+ of the global diabetes population. Without explicit equity pricing strategies, benefits will accrue to wealthy populations only.

C. Preliminary Recommendations

  1. Trial site expansion to India, Mexico, Japan, Turkey. These countries have high diabetes burden and existing clinical trial infrastructure. Expansion would improve representativeness and access.
  2. Regional manufacturing partnerships (India, Brazil, China). This reduces logistics costs and enables tiered pricing aligned with national income levels.
  3. Prioritize encapsulated device approaches (VX-264, Cell Pouch). These may be more scalable to lower-resource settings than portal vein infusion requiring interventional radiology.
  4. Generic/biosimilar pathway planning should begin now. Stem cell manufacturing protocols should be designed for open licensing, especially for lower-income countries.
  5. Embed equity metrics in clinical trial design. Demographic representativeness and accessibility assessment should be endpoints, not afterthoughts.

Methodology & Data Sources

Trial Data Source

ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, queried 2026-03-15. Query: "stem cell" OR "beta cell" OR "islet" AND diabetes. Yielded 29 trials with 211 facility locations across 26 countries. Data includes trial phase, status (recruiting/active), sponsor, enrollment, and facility city/country.

Diabetes Prevalence

IDF Diabetes Atlas 11th Edition (2024), cited prevalence and case counts. Total diabetes estimates: 589M globally. Type 1 diabetes estimates: 9.5M globally (IDF T1D Index v3.0, 2025). Country-level T1D estimates allocated proportionally to 9.5M global figure based on known regional distributions (USA ~1.6M, India ~0.9M, China ~1.2M, Brazil ~0.3M, UK ~0.4M, Germany ~0.3M, Russia ~0.3M, Canada ~0.2M).

Manufacturer Data

Compiled from company filings (SEC 10-K/10-Q for US companies), press releases, and clinical trial registrations. Therapy stages and mechanisms as of March 2026. HQ and manufacturing locations verified via company websites and recent announcements.

Cost Estimates

Manufacturing cost estimates ($50-100M) based on literature review of cGMP cell therapy manufacturing. Initial therapy pricing (~$500K) estimated by analogy to CAR-T therapies (Yescarta/Kymriah market prices 2025). Source: PMID:21323736, Frontiers Cell Dev Biol 2025.

Mismatch Index Calculation

Simple ratio: Diabetes Burden (millions) / (Trial Sites + 0.1). This is NOT a validated epidemiological metric. The +0.1 term prevents division by zero for countries with zero trial sites. Used here as exploratory visualization of supply-demand alignment.

Regional Aggregation

WHO/UN regional definitions. Diabetes burden summed from country-level data. Trial sites counted from facility country tags in ClinicalTrials.gov data.

Validation Status: BRONZE

This analysis is based on single data sources with limited cross-validation. Recommendations for improvement:

  • Expert validation of supply chain assumptions from manufacturers
  • Sub-national data (state/province level) for diabetes prevalence and trial capacity
  • Health system capacity assessment beyond trial sites (interventional radiology, transplant surgery, ICU)
  • Cost modeling with scale-up scenarios (manufacturing expansion, supply chain maturation)
  • Patent and IP landscape analysis (who controls future manufacturing?)

Limitations

  • Trial site count does not equal clinical capacity. Some sites may have limited infrastructure.
  • Diabetes prevalence data is ~1-2 years old; real-time data would be more accurate.
  • T1D estimates are proportional allocations, not based on measured epidemiology in each country.
  • Manufacturer list focuses on later-stage programs; early-stage companies may be omitted.
  • Cost estimates are US-centric and may not reflect manufacturing costs in other regions.
